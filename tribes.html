The Sacrifices & Benefits

As a member of the Lummi tribe, we want to remove the dam. Removing the dam would increase rates for our salmon and we would get our sacred land back. The river was not just a source of water and food it was a way of life. Many of our tribes’ members feel the same way. Although we don’t get our sacred land back, we must make a compromise. By modifying the dams our salmon population will increase and the government has agreed to expand our reservations, resulting in the tribes gaining land. Although this is not a perfect solution for us, we must make sure that other groups are also satisfied. In conclusion this is a sufficient solution.













What The Modification Means To Us

Being a tribe member, modification doesn’t fully fit what we would like, the government has given us an increased land reservation size. Although this is not the best solution for us, we still must compromise and make sure that other people have a stake in the dam as well.
